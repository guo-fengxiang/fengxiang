Welcome! I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Urban and Environmental Sociology at UFZ (since 04/2021), and the Faculty of Physics and Earth Sciences, University of Leipzig (since 02/2020). My thesis title is ‘How urban morphology affects land surface temperature’, under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Uwe Schlink, funded by China Scholarship Council (CSC).

My methodology focuses on monitoring urban dynamics in 3D space as well as urban microclimate using remote sensing technology. A focal point is to understand how urban morphology, particularly the built forms affect the urban heat environment across scales, seasons, and cities. Related studies include land surface temperature downscaling, surface energy balance, 3D urban morphological indicators design, urban heat islands decomposition, and a large-scale comparison of urban morphology across countries. I have published papers in leading journals such as the Remote Sensing of Environment, the Landscape and Urban Planning, the Sustainable Cities and Society, and the Building and Environment.
